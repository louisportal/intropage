The Councillor for French Citizens Abroad is a locally elected representative, representing French nationals living outside France. Elected by direct universal suffrage for a 6-year term, they sit on the Consular Council.

Their main missions:

  • Represent French citizens in their consular constituency
  • Advise on consular matters (school grants, social assistance, etc.)
  • Serve as a key point of contact between French citizens and the consular administration
  • Participate in the Assembly of French Citizens Abroad (AFE)

The Consular Council meets at least twice a year under the chairmanship of the ambassador or consul general. It addresses matters related to social welfare, French education, security and other issues affecting the daily lives of French citizens abroad.
